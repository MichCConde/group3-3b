Wearable Health Tech


Eco-friendly Materials in Health Monitoring Devices

The future of health monitoring is not just smart, but also green. Innovations in wearable health technology are increasingly incorporating eco-friendly materials, minimizing environmental impact while maximizing health benefits. These devices, such as fitness trackers and medical sensors, are being made from biodegradable or recyclable materials, reducing electronic waste. Additionally, some wearables are powered by renewable energy sources like solar cells, further decreasing their carbon footprint. These advancements ensure that while we monitor our health, we also care for the planet.

Biodegradable Materials:

Researchers at the University of Sussex have developed sensors using natural ingredients like seaweed, rock salt, and water, combined with graphene. This innovation results in an electrically conductive hydrogel that can replace traditional polymers in activity trackers. These biodegradable sensors are effective and reduce the risk of microplastics leaching into the environment as they degrade

Sustainable Materials:

Many eco-friendly fitness trackers now use materials such as recycled plastics, organic fabrics, bamboo, and eco-plastic composites. These materials not only lessen the carbon footprint of the devices but also ensure they are gentle on the environment​

Bellabeat's Leaf Series:

This line of activity tracking jewelry includes devices made from sustainable materials like wood and eco-plastic composites. The Leaf Nature, for example, uses white ash wood or African Blackwood, emphasizing a natural and eco-friendly design​. It discreetly tracks activity levels, sleep patterns, menstrual cycles, and stress levels.​

Smart Water Systems

Innovations in Water Purification and Recycling Technologies

Water is a critical resource in healthcare, and emerging smart water systems are at the forefront of ensuring its sustainable use. Innovations in water purification technologies are enabling healthcare facilities to provide clean, safe water while reducing waste. Advanced filtration systems, such as those using nanotechnology, can remove contaminants more efficiently than ever before. Meanwhile, water recycling systems are being implemented to reuse water in various non-critical applications, conserving this precious resource. These smart systems not only improve water quality but also contribute to significant cost savings and environmental protection.

NanotechPure Water Purifier

The NanotechPure Water Purifier is an innovative product that utilizes advanced filtration systems based on nanotechnology to deliver clean and safe drinking water. Equipped with nano-sized pores, the filtration membrane efficiently removes impurities such as bacteria, viruses, microplastics, and heavy metals from the water, ensuring superior purification performance.

Biophilic Design

Integrating Natural Elements into Healthcare Architecture for Better Health Outcomes

Biophilic design is an innovative approach to architecture that incorporates natural elements into built environments, and its application in healthcare is showing promising results. By integrating features such as natural lighting, indoor plants, green walls, and water features, biophilic design creates soothing, healing environments for patients and staff. Research has shown that exposure to natural elements can reduce stress, improve mood, and even accelerate recovery times for patients. In addition, biophilic design enhances air quality and energy efficiency, making healthcare facilities more sustainable and pleasant to inhabit.

Khoo Teck Puat Hospital, Singapore

Infused with natural elements like lush greenery, abundant natural light, and calming water features, KTPH offers patients and staff a healing environment that fosters well-being and accelerates recovery. This innovative approach to design not only enhances the aesthetic appeal of the hospital but also promotes physical and mental health benefits.
